# references

Uploaded texts are authoritative for this project. Search them first before answering a conceptual or pedagogical question, and prefer them over general knowledge. Where the set has no source for a topic, flag the gap and offer to draft a handout or find suitable material rather than filling it in.

Two titles are split across two PDFs each (Auner, and the Tymoczko handouts). Search both parts.

## notation

- Stone, *Music Notation in the Twentieth Century*: contemporary notational practice, the reference for notating extended and twentieth-century techniques.
- Berklee Press, *Music Notation: Preparing Scores and Parts*: practical score and part preparation, layout, and engraving conventions.

## orchestration

- Polansky, *Orchestration Outline and Bibliography*: an orchestration course outline and reading list.
- Salzedo, *Modern Study of the Harp*: harp technique, effects, and idiomatic writing.
- `Instrument_Ranges`, `Strings`, `WindsTranspositionChart`, `Voice_Ranges`: quick-reference charts for ranges and transposition across the orchestra and voices.

## harmony and pitch organization

- Persichetti, *Twentieth-Century Harmony*: harmonic materials and practice of the twentieth century.
- Russell, *The Lydian Chromatic Concept of Tonal Organization*: modal and chromatic organization around tonal gravity.
- Felts, *Reharmonization Techniques*: reharmonization approaches, jazz-grounded.

## analysis and process

- Tymoczko, *Handouts* (parts 1 and 2): voice leading, chord and scale space, the geometry of common-practice and chromatic materials.
- Beach, *Advanced Schenkerian Analysis*: Schenkerian analysis with attention to phrase rhythm, motive, and form.
- Pankhurst, *SchenkerGUIDE*: a working guide to Schenkerian analysis.
- Burnett, *Composition, Chromaticism and the Developmental Process*: chromaticism and thematic and developmental process.
- Maor, *Music by the Numbers*: the mathematics behind music, Pythagoras through Schoenberg.

## counterpoint and rhythm

- Kennan, *Counterpoint*: tonal counterpoint, eighteenth-century practice.
- Smith, *Rhythm*: rhythmic organization and practice.

## history and context

- Griffiths, *Modern Music and After*: postwar and contemporary music.
- Griffiths, *A Concise History of Western Music*: the broad historical arc.
- Ross, *The Rest Is Noise*: a narrative listening history of the twentieth century.
- Auner, *Music in the Twentieth and Twenty-First Centuries* (parts 1 and 2): survey of modern and contemporary repertoire and context.

## gaps to be aware of

The orchestration backbone here is Polansky's outline, the range and transposition charts, and Salzedo for the harp, with no comprehensive orchestration treatise in the set. Notation is covered for twentieth-century practice (Stone) and for score and part preparation (Berklee Press), with no general engraving manual. When a topic needs one of these and the set comes up short, flag it and offer to draft or source material.
