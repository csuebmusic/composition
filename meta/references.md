# references

Uploaded texts are authoritative for this project. Search them first before answering a conceptual or pedagogical question, and prefer them over general knowledge. Where the set has no source for a topic, flag the gap and offer to draft a handout or find suitable material rather than filling it in.

Availability splits two ways. Items marked "in project" are uploaded here and searchable directly. Items marked "Canvas" are in the students' Canvas set but not uploaded here, so they can't be searched directly; when a topic needs one, we pull the relevant passage while building. The scores are all on Canvas.

Two in-project titles are split across two PDFs each (Auner, and the Tymoczko handouts). Search both parts.

## textbooks

### notation
- Stone, *Music Notation in the Twentieth Century*: contemporary notational practice, the reference for notating extended and twentieth-century techniques. (in project)
- Berklee Press, *Music Notation: Preparing Scores and Parts*: practical score and part preparation, layout, and engraving conventions. (in project)
- Gould, *Behind Bars*: the comprehensive modern engraving and notation manual. (Canvas)

### orchestration
- Adler, *The Study of Orchestration*: the standard comprehensive orchestration text. (Canvas)
- *The Cambridge Guide to Orchestration*: reference on orchestration technique and practice. (Canvas)
- Solomon, *How to Write for Percussion*: writing idiomatically for percussion. (Canvas)
- Polansky, *Orchestration Outline and Bibliography*: an orchestration course outline and reading list. (in project)
- Salzedo, *Modern Study of the Harp*: harp technique, effects, and idiomatic writing. (in project)

### harmony and pitch organization
- Persichetti, *Twentieth-Century Harmony*: harmonic materials and practice of the twentieth century. (in project)
- Russell, *The Lydian Chromatic Concept of Tonal Organization*: modal and chromatic organization around tonal gravity. (in project)
- Felts, *Reharmonization Techniques*: reharmonization approaches, jazz-grounded. (in project)

### analysis and process
- Tymoczko, *Handouts* (parts 1 and 2): voice leading, chord and scale space, the geometry of common-practice and chromatic materials. (in project)
- Beach, *Advanced Schenkerian Analysis*: Schenkerian analysis with attention to phrase rhythm, motive, and form. (in project)
- Pankhurst, *SchenkerGUIDE*: a working guide to Schenkerian analysis. (in project)
- Burnett, *Composition, Chromaticism and the Developmental Process*: chromaticism and thematic and developmental process. (in project)
- Maor, *Music by the Numbers*: the mathematics behind music, Pythagoras through Schoenberg. (in project)

### counterpoint and rhythm
- Kennan, *Counterpoint*: tonal counterpoint, eighteenth-century practice. (in project)
- Smith, *Rhythm*: rhythmic organization and practice. (in project)

### history and context
- Griffiths, *Modern Music and After*: postwar and contemporary music. (in project)
- Griffiths, *A Concise History of Western Music*: the broad historical arc. (in project)
- Ross, *The Rest Is Noise*: a narrative listening history of the twentieth century. (in project)
- Auner, *Music in the Twentieth and Twenty-First Centuries* (parts 1 and 2): survey of modern and contemporary repertoire and context. (in project)

## gaps

The Canvas set covers both: Adler and the Cambridge Guide for orchestration, Gould for engraving, and Solomon for percussion writing. They aren't searchable in the project, so when a topic leans on them, we pull the relevant passage while building.
